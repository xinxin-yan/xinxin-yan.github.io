1. Interpretation of Similes and Metaphors

This project investigates how people comprehend figurative comparisons such as similes and metaphors. It uses eye-tracking and reaction-time experiments to compare processing patterns, aiming to distinguish literal vs. figurative mechanisms of interpretation.

  • Presentation: Relevance Researcher’s Network Conference, May 2025
  • Manuscript in preparation

2. Multiword Metaphor Detection (NLP)

An ongoing NLP project focused on developing models that can automatically detect extended metaphor and simile expressions in natural language. This work bridges computational and theoretical perspectives on figurative language.

  • Project lead: Xinxin
  • Tools: Python, NLP frameworks
  • Publication planned

3. Literal vs. Non-literal Use in Pragmatics Teaching

Part of the UCL short course module “Literal” Expressions and Their Uses, this project explores how literalness is taught and conceptualized, based on co-designed teaching materials and student feedback.

  • Teaching module: Jan 2025 (co-taught with Teru and Yolanda)
  • Reflections and examples featured in course materials
