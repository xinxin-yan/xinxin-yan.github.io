1. Interpretation of Comparison statements, Similes and Metaphors

My doctoral research focused on developing a comprehensive, cognitively plausible pragmatic account of comparison statement comprehension (e.g., ‘X is like a Y’), with a strong emphasis on the interpretation of similes (e.g. ‘John is like a cactus’). I approached this problem from both theoretical and experimental perspectives.

2. Multiword Metaphor Detection (NLP)

An ongoing NLP project focused on developing models that can automatically detect extended metaphor and simile expressions in natural language. This work bridges computational and theoretical perspectives on figurative language.

  • Project lead: Xinxin
  • Tools: Python, NLP frameworks

3. Metaphor and Metonymy – Referring and Predicating – Costs and Benefits

This project explores how speakers use metaphor and metonymy to communicate meaning beyond the literal, and how listeners interpret these figurative expressions in context. Using reading time measures and eye-tracking, we investigate the cognitive costs and benefits of metaphors and metonymies when used referentially or predicatively. Our findings aim to reveal whether metaphors are more effective in predication and metonymies in reference, contributing to a deeper theoretical understanding of figurative language use.

  • Carston, R., & Yan, X. (2023). Metaphor processing: Referring and predicating. Cognition, 238, 105534. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cognition.2023.105534
